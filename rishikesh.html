Discover Rishikesh

About

Rishikesh, often referred to as the "Yoga Capital of the World," is a serene town located in the foothills of the Himalayas along the banks of the sacred Ganges River. Known for its spiritual significance, Rishikesh attracts pilgrims, yoga enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike.

Attractions

  • Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula: Iconic suspension bridges that connect the two banks of the Ganges
  • Triveni Ghat: A sacred bathing ghat where visitors can participate in the evening Ganga Aarti
  • Shivpuri: A popular spot for adventure activities such as river rafting, trekking, and camping along the Ganges.
  • Neer Garh Waterfall: A picturesque waterfall located near Laxman Jhula, ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit is from September to April.
  • Book adventure activities like rafting or trekking in advance during peak seasons to ensure availability.
  • Rishikesh is well-connected by road; consider hiring a taxi or using local buses for easy travel.
  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and ghats, respecting local customs.

Accommodations

  • Aloha on the Ganges: A luxurious riverside resort.
  • Hotel Shivangi: A budget-friendly option .
  • BrijRama Palace: A heritage hotel that provides a royal experience
  • Parmarth Niketan Ashram: Offers simple accommodations along with yoga classes and spiritual programs.
