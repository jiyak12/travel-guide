Discover Delhi

About

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly blends ancient history with modernity. Known as a city of contrasts, it has been the center of power for various empires throughout history, including the Delhi Sultanate and the Mughal Empire.

Attractions

  • Red Fort: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this magnificent fort is an iconic symbol of India's rich history.
  • Qutub Minar: The tallest brick minaret in the world, showcasing exquisite Indo-Islamic architecture.
  • Humayun's Tomb: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, this tomb is a stunning example of Mughal architecture.
  • India Gate: A war memorial dedicated to Indian soldiers who died in World War I, surrounded by lush gardens.
  • Jama Masjid: One of the largest mosques in India, known for its impressive courtyard and stunning architecture.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit is from October to March.
  • Don't miss trying street food like chaat, parathas from Paranthe Wali Gali, and local delicacies like butter chicken.
  • Use the Delhi Metro for efficient travel; it connects major attractions and areas.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and be respectful of local customs.
